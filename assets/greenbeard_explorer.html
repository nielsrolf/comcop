Green-beard simulation — tag-based kin recognition, random pairing

Every agent carries a costless, heritable 16-bit tag (shown as its color) and a heritable tolerance $\tau$. It cooperates with a co-player only if their tags match on a fraction $\ge \tau$ of bits. Pairing is uniformly random — there is no spatial structure — so any cooperation that survives comes purely from reading the tag. Runs auto-loop; click any agent to inspect it.

Click an agent to inspect its tag, tolerance, and how similar the rest of the population is to it.
Configure
Per round, 2 players: mutual cooperate +1/+1, mutual defect 0/0, one-sided defect gives the defector +2 and the cooperator −1 (matches games.py). Configuring anything stops the auto-loop.
